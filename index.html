The dataset all_seasons contains NBA player statistics from 1996 through 2023. Per the project requirements, this analysis is restricted to seasons 2005–06 through 2021–22. Within that window are statistics such as position, points, rebounds, assists, and net rating.

Position is derived from player height (Guard <196cm, Forward 196–210cm, Center >210cm). The purpose of this project is to use visualization to surface statistical trends in the NBA over this period.

All charts below are rendered client-side from the original all_seasons.csv dataset — the same source data and aggregation logic used in the R analysis.

02Age & Performance

How does performance change with age, and who excelled late?

Average NBA Performance by Player Age

Players Who Excelled After Age 35  (min 25 GP & 15 pts/game)

In general, scoring increases as athletes begin their careers and peaks around age 28. After that, scoring declines overall — with exceptions. LeBron James averaged over 25 points per game past age 35. Rebounds and assists stay relatively stable, with a slight increase as athletes age.

League-wide scoring has not changed significantly over time — a small upward trend of almost 1 point per game. The top scorer each season, however, has increased by almost 4 points, led by Kevin Durant's four seasons atop the league.

04Rookie Impact

How do rookies compare to prime and veteran players?

Average Performance by Career Stage

Top Performing Rookies  (min 20 GP)

Both veteran and prime players perform significantly better than rookies in all stat categories. Veterans slightly outpace prime players in rebounds, assists, and scoring — suggesting that as players age, they take on a larger helping role than they had in their prime.

05Positional Analysis

Which positions lead in scoring, rebounding, and assists?

Average Stats by Position (All Seasons)

Average Points per Game by Position Over Time

Centers have increased in point scoring over time, forwards have slightly decreased, and guards have stayed roughly the same — scoring has shifted away from forwards and toward centers and guards. Guards lead in assists and points but rank last in rebounds; centers lead in rebounding, closest to the basket.

06Player Efficiency

What does net rating reveal about player and league efficiency?

League-Wide Average Net Rating Over Time

Top Players by Career Net Rating  (min 8 seasons, 20+ GP)

Average net rating has stayed relatively constant over time. The players with the highest career net ratings — led by Kawhi Leonard and Manu Ginobili — are evenly distributed between current and past players, suggesting no major shift in on-court efficiency over this period.

07International Impact

How has international representation evolved?

International Players as % of NBA Roster

Player-Seasons by Country (excl. USA)

Top International Scorers  (min 5 seasons, 20+ GP)

There has been a steady increase in international players in the NBA, led by Canada, France, and Australia. Most top countries by representation are wealthier nations — the clear exception being Joel Embiid's Cameroon, currently the league's top international scorer. No country repeats among the top-scoring internationals, and there's no obvious link between a country's representation and the caliber of its top players.
