The dataset E-commerce Customer Behavior.csv provides descriptive statistics on customer behavior. The data measure gender, quantity, satisfaction, total spend, and customers' ages. There are 350 total observations. 2 of the observations contained NA for the satisfaction level. These observations were filtered out of the charts which showed satisfaction. The purpose of this exercise is to use various visuals to model the data for an e-commerce site.

Write-up

Analysis

The bronze membership has the lowest variation, along with the lowest average. The gold membership has the highest variation, along with the highest average. The silver membership is in the middle for both variation and median. The Gold membership has a median near its own 25th percentile, which indicates that there are high outliers. The Silver membership median is near its 75th percentile, which indicates that there are low outliers.

There is a strong positive correlation between items purchased and total spend, as shown by the r = 0.972.

San Francisco has the highest mean customer rating, while Houston has the lowest. New York and Los Angeles also have mean scores above 4, while Miami and Chicago have scores below 4.

There is a relatively weak negative correlation of −0.54 between days since last purchase and total spend.

With no discount, the variation of total spend is higher, along with a slightly higher mean spend. With a discount, the mean is slightly lower, and so is the variation.

The bronze membership shows values between 34 and 43 years old with a gap between 38 and 40. The gold membership is relatively unimodal and centered at 30 years old, with a distribution between 28 and 34 years old. The silver membership shows a bimodal distribution between 25 and 36 years old.

There is a relatively strong positive correlation between customer satisfaction level and total spending, as shown by the r = 0.797.

In general, women spend less than men on this site. The variations are relatively similar, and both medians are close to their 25th percentiles. This indicates that the spending variations are relatively similar across the two genders; however, males spend more on average.

The cities with high satisfaction scores also have higher total quantities. Miami has a higher mean than Los Angeles, Chicago, and Houston, but the variation is significantly large, which means the order can fluctuate for Miami and Los Angeles depending on the observation.
